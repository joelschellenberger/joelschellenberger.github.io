Committing to accessible, inclusive design on this website

joelschellenberger.com/blog/inclusive-design
If you had to summarize the field of user experience design into one word, the word most users would choose is "empathy." At it's core, the discipline and adjacent roles are built on the foundation that by being empathetic toward users, we are able to best serve those users and provide an optimal experience.

Obviously, the ethical considerations of the modern technology industry are constantly being evaluated and criticized. I personally find this criticism both fair and necessary to ensure that we are properly addressing people's needs while protecting and supporting our fellow human beings.

As someone who takes pride in working in a field so focused on empathy, I felt it was important to outline the steps that I take and am committed to taking to make this website as accessible as possible. When it comes to ethics, I feel a particular personal responsibility to commit to human-centred design choices while constructing this website.

Maybe it's my philsophy minor talking, but considering as many users as possible and building something that works well for everyone just seems like the right thing to do.
This post is meant to serve as a public commitment to accessible design. Below, you'll find specific actions that I have committed to taking in the future to ensure I'm building an experience respectful of the unique challenges that folks have. I'll also outline a timeline for these specific actions.
I have pledged to adhere to the following ethical sensibilities while creating content for this website:
  • Create a website that is built to be accessible (more on the specifics below)
  • Create a website that scales regardless of the device a user is visiting from
  • Reduce/optimize the file sizes of images to avoid large amounts of bandwidth usage
  • Host the website on GitHub, with source files and code available to view
  • Publish and maintain a changelog that informs visitors of progress being made
  • Include diverse illustrations with the goal of representing a wide range of backgrounds, cultures, ethnicities, and gender identities
  • Acknowledge the traditional land that each project was built on

If you're cureious about what "built to be accessible" means in practice, here are some examples of decisions made with accessibility best practices in mind:
  • Keep reading level to grade 12 or below (verified using the Hemingway app)
  • Check that text is set on appropriate contrast (verified using WCAG colour checker)
  • Properly tagging all images for users who use assistive devices
  • Keep font size large (18px or higher)
  • Keep line spacing large (1.6x font size in most places)

November 2020

  • Launch joelschellenberger.com (Portfolio v3.0)
  • All net-new pages must adhere to new accessibility standards

January 2021

  • Retro-actively correct all pages to meet new accessibility standard (including alt-tagging images and properly formatted articles)

April 2021

  • Complete an accessibility audit to identify areas of improvement
  • Continue to develop additional content that promotes accessible and inclusive design practices

I want to acknowledge that this website was built on territories traditionally occupied and cared for by Indigenous Peoples. I attended Wilfrid Laurier University's Brantford campus in an area traditionally occupied by Anishinabeg, Haudenosaunee and Neutral peoples. I want to express my gratitude for the opportunity to learn and grow on this land, which has been cared for by the First Peoples of Canada. We must continue to care about the land and about the issues facing Indigenous peoples.
